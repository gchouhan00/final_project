Rules while playing Chaupar

Since, all games have some basic rules to play them so do the Chaupar. These are discussed below:

  • Three long dice are required. Each die has 1 and 6 on opposing faces & 2 and 5 on the other faces
  • There are no graces or extra throws
  • The pieces start on specific square instead of the charkoni although captured pieces are returned to the charkoni
    To prepare to start the game, position each set of four pieces on squares 6, 7, 23 and 24
  • A throw cannot be passed in whole part or unless a player cannot move.
  • Each throw can be split into its constituent parts and shared across the pieces
  • Conglomerate pieces move using the throw of the dice as if they were single piece
  • A double piece can only be captured by a double, triple or quadruple piece, a triple piece is only
    vulnerable to a triple or quadruple pieces and a quadruple piece can only be captured by another quadruple piece.
  • An exact throw is required for a piece to get home
